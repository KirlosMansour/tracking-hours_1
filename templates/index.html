📖 Glossary & How EAC Works

Terms & Equations

BAC — Planned Hours (Budget at Completion)
The total hours that were originally planned/budgeted for that line item before work started. This is your fixed baseline — it does not change as the project progresses.
📥 Manual entry — no formula. Set once at the start.
AC — Actual Hours
The real hours that have actually been spent on that line item so far. Updated continuously as work happens.
📥 Manual entry — updated regularly as hours are logged.
% Complete
Your honest estimate of how much of that line item's work is actually finished. This is a judgement call — it should reflect true progress, not just time elapsed.
📥 Manual entry — value between 0 and 100.
ETC — Estimate to Complete
A fresh, hands-on estimate of how many hours are still needed to finish the remaining work, given directly by whoever is doing the work. Optional — if you leave it blank the app calculates EAC using Method 1 or 2 instead.
📥 Manual entry (optional) — provided by the team based on current knowledge.
EV — Earned Hours (Earned Value)
The portion of the planned hours that your progress actually represents. It answers "how many hours worth of work have we actually completed?" — which is different from how many hours we've spent.
EV = BAC × (% Complete ÷ 100)
Example: BAC = 400 hrs, 50% complete → EV = 400 × 0.50 = 200 hrs
CPI — Cost Performance Index
An efficiency ratio — are you getting a full hour of progress for every hour spent? CPI above 1 means you're ahead of plan; below 1 means it's costing more hours than expected. If no actual hours have been logged yet, CPI shows N/A.
CPI = EV ÷ AC
Example: EV = 200 hrs, AC = 250 hrs → CPI = 200 ÷ 250 = 0.80 (20% less efficient than planned)
EAC — Estimate at Completion
The projected total hours that line item will take once it's fully finished — combining the hours already spent with a forecast of what's still needed. Calculated three different ways depending on what assumption you want to make (see methods below).
Variance
How far off the project is projected to finish compared to the original budget. Positive = over budget; zero or negative = on or under budget. Calculated using whichever EAC method the app selects for that row.
Variance = EAC − BAC
Example: EAC = 420 hrs, BAC = 400 hrs → Variance = 420 − 400 = +20 hrs over budget
Overall % Complete (Summary card)
The project-level percent complete — weighted by planned hours so larger tasks have more impact on the number than smaller ones.
Overall % Complete = Total EV ÷ Total BAC × 100
Example: Total EV = 1094 hrs, Total BAC = 3401 hrs → 1094 ÷ 3401 × 100 = 32.2%
Overall CPI (Summary card)
The project-level efficiency ratio — same idea as row-level CPI but computed from the totals across all rows.
Overall CPI = Total EV ÷ Total AC
Example: Total EV = 1094, Total AC = 709 → 1094 ÷ 709 = 1.54
Total EAC (Summary card)
The sum of each row's own EAC (whichever method was used for that row). This is not simply BAC ÷ Overall CPI — it's a row-by-row total, since different rows may use different EAC methods.
Total EAC = Σ EAC(used) for every row

The three EAC methods

Method 1 — "If we keep going at this pace"

Assumes your current efficiency (CPI) will continue for the rest of the task. Use when slow or fast progress so far reflects a real, ongoing pattern — not a one-time fluke.

EAC₁ = BAC ÷ CPI
Example: BAC = 400, CPI = 0.80 → EAC₁ = 400 ÷ 0.80 = 500 hrs
Method 2 — "Assume it was a one-time hiccup"

Assumes whatever caused the variance so far is behind you, and the rest of the work will go exactly as originally planned from here on. Use when the issue has been fixed and won't repeat.

EAC₂ = AC + (BAC − EV)
Example: AC = 250, BAC = 400, EV = 200 → EAC₂ = 250 + (400 − 200) = 450 hrs
Method 3 — "Ask the team directly"

Instead of relying on a trend or formula, someone close to the work gives a fresh bottom-up estimate of hours remaining (ETC). Usually the most accurate when good first-hand judgment is available.

EAC₃ = AC + ETC
Example: AC = 250, ETC = 180 (team's estimate) → EAC₃ = 250 + 180 = 430 hrs

Which method does the app use?
If ETC is entered → Method 3 is used (human re-estimate takes priority).
If ETC is blank but AC > 0 → Method 1 is used (trend-based).
If both ETC is blank and AC = 0 → Method 2 is used (original plan as fallback).
