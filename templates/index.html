📖 Glossary & How EAC Works

Terms & Equations

BAC — Planned Hours (Budget at Completion)
The total hours that were originally planned/budgeted for that line item before work started. This is your fixed baseline — it does not change as the project progresses.
📥 Manual entry — no formula. Set once at the start.
AC — Actual Hours
The real hours that have actually been spent on that line item so far. Updated continuously as work happens — either manually or via the Time Log.
📥 Manual entry or Time Log — updated regularly as hours are logged.
% Complete
Your honest estimate of how much of that line item's work is actually finished. This is a judgement call — it should reflect true progress, not just time elapsed.
📥 Manual entry — value between 0 and 100.
ETC — Estimate to Complete
A fresh, hands-on estimate of how many hours are still needed to finish the remaining work, given directly by whoever is doing the work. Optional — if you leave it blank the app calculates EAC using Method 1 or 2 instead.
📥 Manual entry (optional) — provided by the team based on current knowledge.
EV — Earned Hours (Earned Value)
The portion of the planned hours that your progress actually represents.
EV = BAC × (% Complete ÷ 100)
Example: BAC = 400 hrs, 50% complete → EV = 400 × 0.50 = 200 hrs
CPI — Cost Performance Index
An efficiency ratio — are you getting a full hour of progress for every hour spent?
CPI = EV ÷ AC
Example: EV = 200 hrs, AC = 250 hrs → CPI = 200 ÷ 250 = 0.80
EAC — Estimate at Completion
The projected total hours that line item will take once fully finished. Calculated three ways depending on your assumption.
Variance
How far off the project is projected to finish versus the original budget.
Variance = EAC − BAC
Example: EAC = 420 hrs, BAC = 400 hrs → Variance = +20 hrs over budget
Overall % Complete (Summary card)
Overall % Complete = Total EV ÷ Total BAC × 100
Overall CPI (Summary card)
Overall CPI = Total EV ÷ Total AC
Total EAC (Summary card)
Total EAC = Σ EAC(used) for every row

The three EAC methods

Method 1 — "If we keep going at this pace"

Assumes your current efficiency (CPI) will continue for the rest of the task.

EAC₁ = BAC ÷ CPI
Example: BAC = 400, CPI = 0.80 → EAC₁ = 400 ÷ 0.80 = 500 hrs
Method 2 — "Assume it was a one-time hiccup"

Assumes the rest of the work will go exactly as originally planned.

EAC₂ = AC + (BAC − EV)
Example: AC = 250, BAC = 400, EV = 200 → EAC₂ = 450 hrs
Method 3 — "Ask the team directly"

Uses a fresh bottom-up estimate of hours remaining (ETC) provided by the team.

EAC₃ = AC + ETC
Example: AC = 250, ETC = 180 → EAC₃ = 430 hrs

Which method does the app use?
If ETC is entered → Method 3. If ETC blank & AC > 0 → Method 1. If both blank → Method 2.
