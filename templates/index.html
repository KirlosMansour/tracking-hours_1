📖 Glossary & How EAC Works

📐 Core EVM (Earned Value Management) Terms

BAC — Budget at Completion (Planned Hours)
The total hours originally planned/budgeted for a task before work started. This is your fixed baseline — it does not change once the project begins.
📥 Manual entry — set once at project start.
AC — Actual Hours
Real hours spent on a task so far. Updated via the table inputs or automatically when hours are logged in the Time Log tab.
📥 Manual entry or Time Log
% Complete
Your estimate of how much of a task is actually finished — a judgement call, not just elapsed time. Should reflect true progress.
📥 Manual entry — 0 to 100.
ETC — Estimate to Complete
A fresh bottom-up estimate of how many hours are still needed to finish the remaining work. Optional — leave blank to let the app forecast automatically using Method 1 or 2.
📥 Manual entry (optional)
EV — Earned Hours (Earned Value)
The portion of planned hours that your actual progress represents. It answers "how many hours worth of work have we truly completed?" — not how many hours were spent.
EV = BAC × (% Complete ÷ 100)
Example: BAC = 400 hrs, 50% complete → EV = 400 × 0.50 = 200 hrs
CPI — Cost Performance Index
Efficiency ratio — how many hours of real progress per hour spent. Above 1 = ahead of plan. Below 1 = it's costing more hours than expected for the same progress.
CPI = EV ÷ AC
Example: EV = 200 hrs, AC = 250 hrs → CPI = 0.80 (20% less efficient than planned)
EAC — Estimate at Completion
The projected total hours a task will take once fully finished — combining hours spent with a forecast of what's still needed. Calculated three ways (see EAC Methods below).
Variance
How far the project is projected to finish versus the original budget. Positive = over budget, negative = under budget.
Variance = EAC − BAC
Example: EAC = 420, BAC = 400 → Variance = +20 hrs over budget
Overall % Complete
Project-level percent done, weighted by planned hours so larger tasks count more.
Overall % Complete = Total EV ÷ Total BAC × 100
Overall CPI
Project-level efficiency ratio across all phase rows combined.
Overall CPI = Total EV ÷ Total AC
Total EAC
Sum of each phase row's individual EAC (whichever Method was used per row). Not simply BAC ÷ Overall CPI, because different rows may use different Methods.
Total EAC = Σ EAC(used per row)

📊 The Three EAC Methods

Method 1 — "If we keep going at this pace"

Assumes your current efficiency (CPI) will continue for the rest of the task. Best used when the variance reflects a real, ongoing pattern.

EAC₁ = BAC ÷ CPI
Example: BAC = 400, CPI = 0.80 → EAC₁ = 400 ÷ 0.80 = 500 hrs
Method 2 — "Assume it was a one-time hiccup"

Assumes the past variance is fixed and remaining work goes exactly as planned. Best when the issue causing the variance has been resolved.

EAC₂ = AC + (BAC − EV)
Example: AC = 250, BAC = 400, EV = 200 → EAC₂ = 450 hrs
Method 3 — "Ask the team directly"

Uses the team's fresh estimate of remaining hours (ETC). Most accurate when reliable first-hand knowledge is available.

EAC₃ = AC + ETC
Example: AC = 250, ETC = 180 → EAC₃ = 430 hrs

Which method does the app use automatically?
ETC entered → Method 3  |  ETC blank & AC > 0 → Method 1  |  Both blank → Method 2.

📅 Schedule & Date Terms

Total Working Days
The total number of weekdays (Monday–Friday only, no weekends) between the project Start Date and the Planned End Date. Saturdays and Sundays are fully excluded from this count.
Total Working Days = count of Mon–Fri between Start Date and Planned End Date
Elapsed Working Days
The number of weekdays that have passed since the project Start Date up to today. Tells you how much working time has gone by so far.
Elapsed Working Days = count of Mon–Fri from Start Date to Today
Working Days Left
The number of weekdays remaining between today and the Planned End Date. Turns yellow when under 10 days, shows "Overdue" if today is past the planned end date.
Working Days Left = count of Mon–Fri from Today to Planned End Date
Planned hrs/day
How many hours per working day your team needs to average to finish all planned work exactly on time. A sanity check — if this number seems unrealistically high for your team size, the schedule may be too tight.
Planned hrs/day = Total BAC ÷ Total Working Days
Example: BAC = 3401 hrs, 219 working days → 15.5 hrs/day needed
Actual hrs/day
How many hours per working day your team has actually been logging on average since the project started. This is the real burn rate — if it is lower than Planned hrs/day you are behind pace.
Actual hrs/day = Total AC ÷ Elapsed Working Days
Example: AC = 709 hrs, 64 elapsed days → 11.1 hrs/day actual
Expected End (Actual Pace)
A forecast of when the project will actually finish if the team continues at their current actual burn rate. Appears as a chip beside the Status by Stage row. Excludes Optional phase hours since those are not core deliverables. Shows green (early) or red (late) compared to the Planned End Date.
Remaining hrs = EAC (core phases only) − AC (core phases only)
Days still needed = Remaining hrs ÷ Actual hrs/day
Expected End = Today + Days still needed (weekdays only)

⚡ Scale & Efficiency Terms

MW — Project Size (Megawatts)
The generation or storage capacity of the project (e.g. 150 MW solar or 200 MW BESS). Entered manually in the project info bar. Used to normalize engineering hours per unit of project size, making it easy to compare different projects.
📥 Manual entry — in the project info bar.
Planned hrs/MW
How many engineering hours were planned per megawatt of project capacity. A benchmark of design complexity — useful for comparing similar projects or estimating future projects of different sizes.
Planned hrs/MW = Total BAC ÷ Project MW
Example: BAC = 3401 hrs, 150 MW → 22.7 hrs/MW planned
Actual hrs/MW
How many engineering hours have actually been spent per megawatt so far. Compare to Planned hrs/MW to see if the project is tracking as expected. Also useful as a benchmark for bidding on future projects of similar size.
Actual hrs/MW = Total AC ÷ Project MW
Example: AC = 709 hrs spent, 150 MW → 4.7 hrs/MW actual so far
Project Type (PV / BESS)
Whether the project is a PV (Photovoltaic / Solar) or BESS (Battery Energy Storage System) project. Stored as a label alongside the MW value. Useful for filtering and comparing hrs/MW benchmarks between project types, since BESS and PV have different engineering complexity profiles.

🔮 Next Project Planning

Next Project Buffer %
A safety margin added on top of the EAC when estimating how many hours to plan for the next similar project. Accounts for unknowns, scope creep, and the fact that EAC alone is already optimistic. Configurable from 5% to 30% using the dropdown above the phases table — defaults to 20%.
📥 Dropdown selection — per project, saved automatically.
Next Project Est. (column)
The recommended hours to plan for each phase on the next similar project, based on this project's performance plus a safety buffer. Shown as the last column in the phases table, highlighted in blue. The total at the bottom of this column is the number to use as your next project's BAC.
Next Project Est. = EAC × (1 + Buffer% ÷ 100)
Example: EAC = 400 hrs, 20% buffer → Next Est. = 400 × 1.20 = 480 hrs
Changing the buffer % instantly updates all rows and the total.

👥 Team & Workload Terms

Team Allocation
The breakdown of planned engineering hours per team member for a specific project. Entered in the "Team Hour Allocation" section at the bottom of each project. The total allocated hours represents the project's planned capacity. Used to calculate each person's share and daily commitment.
📥 Manual entry — member + hours per project.
Share %
The percentage of the project's total allocated hours that a specific team member is responsible for. Tells you how much of the project belongs to each person.
Share % = Member Allocated Hours ÷ Total Allocated Hours × 100
Example: Sriram has 300 hrs out of 900 total → 33.3% share
Full Hrs/Day
The daily hour commitment required from a team member to complete their allocated hours within the project's working day count. Based purely on the allocation and project dates — no scaling applied.
Full Hrs/Day = Member Allocated Hours ÷ Project Working Days
Example: 300 hrs ÷ 219 days = 1.37 hrs/day
Workload % (per person per project)
A scaling factor (0–100%) that reduces a person's effective daily commitment on a project. Set to 100% by default — meaning they work at full allocated rate. Reduce it when someone is part-time on a project, on leave, or their focus has temporarily shifted. Affects the Effective Hrs/Day only, not the original allocation.
📥 Manual entry — 0 to 100%, editable in the Team Allocation table.
Effective Hrs/Day
The actual daily hours a person is committing to a project after applying their Workload % scaling. This is what's used in the Team Workload tab to calculate total daily commitment and availability.
Effective Hrs/Day = Full Hrs/Day × (Workload% ÷ 100)
Example: Full 1.37 hrs/day × 80% workload = 1.10 eff.hrs/day
Current Workload (Team tab)
A snapshot of each team member's active project commitments on a specific date. Shows their total committed hours per day, available hours remaining in the working day (standard: 8 hrs), and which projects are contributing to that commitment. Only active, non-closed projects where the selected date falls on or after the start date are counted. Overdue projects (past their end date but not closed) still appear.
Project Status — Active / Closed
A project is Active by default — it counts toward workload as long as it has started (even if overdue). Clicking "Close Project" in the project header marks it as Closed, which immediately removes it from all workload calculations. Use this button when a project is genuinely finished. Closed projects can be reopened at any time.
Resource Gap
The difference between a project's total EAC and the total hours allocated to team members. A positive gap means there aren't enough allocated team hours to cover the forecast — you may need to add more people or hours. A negative gap means more capacity is allocated than the forecast needs.
Resource Gap = Project EAC − Total Allocated Team Hours
Example: EAC = 3154 hrs, Allocated = 2800 hrs → Gap = +354 hrs short

📈 Forecasting Terms

Team Forecasting
A person-level projection of how many hours each team member will actually need to finish their share of each project, based on the project's current performance (CPI) and how many hours they have already logged. Answers: "Is this person going to finish on time? Are they over-committed?"
Person EV (Earned Value per person)
The share of planned hours a person has "earned" based on the project's overall progress. If the project is 40% done, a person planned for 300 hours has earned 120 of those hours — regardless of how many they've actually logged.
Person EV = Person BAC × (Project Overall % Complete ÷ 100)
Example: 300 hrs planned, project 40% done → Person EV = 120 hrs
Person CPI
The efficiency ratio for an individual on a project — how much earned value per hour actually logged. Below 1 means they're spending more hours than the project's progress justifies for their share; above 1 means they're contributing more efficiently than planned.
Person CPI = Person EV ÷ Person AC
Example: EV = 120 hrs, AC = 150 hrs logged → Person CPI = 0.80
Person EAC (Forecasted Total per person)
The total hours a person is projected to spend on the project once it's fully complete. Uses Method 1 (trend continues) if they have logged actual hours, or Method 2 (one-time issue) if they haven't yet.
Person EAC = Person BAC ÷ Person CPI   (Method 1)
Person EAC = Person AC + (Person BAC − Person EV)   (Method 2)
Example: BAC = 300, CPI = 0.80 → Person EAC = 300 ÷ 0.80 = 375 hrs
Remaining Hours (person)
How many more hours a person needs to log to finish their share of the project, based on their forecasted total (EAC) minus what they've already logged.
Remaining = Person EAC − Person AC
Example: EAC = 375 hrs, AC = 150 hrs logged → 225 hrs remaining
Forecasted Finish Date (per person)
When a specific team member is projected to complete their share of a project, based on their remaining hours and their effective daily rate. Weekends excluded. Shown with a ▲ (early) or ▼ (late) indicator versus the project's planned end date.
Days needed = Remaining hrs ÷ Effective hrs/day
Forecast End = Today + Days needed (weekdays only)
Capacity Risk
A warning that appears on a team member's forecasting card when their total effective hrs/day across all active projects exceeds 8 hours — the standard working day. This means the person is over-committed and either needs their workload reduced, projects closed/delayed, or their allocation on one project reduced.
